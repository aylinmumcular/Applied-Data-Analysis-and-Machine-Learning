Challenger disaster

The 1986 crash of the space shuttle Challenger was linked to failure of O-ring seals in the rocket engines. Data was collected on the 23 previous shuttle missions. The launch temperature on the day of the crash was 31C (J. Faraway citing Presidential Commission on the Space Shuttle Challenger Accident, Vol. 1, 1986: 129-131.)
